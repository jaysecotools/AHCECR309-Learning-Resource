Student User Guide – AHCECR309: Conduct an Ecological and Cultural Site Inspection

Purpose of the Tool

The AHCECR309 Learning Resource is designed to help you gain the knowledge and skills needed to carry out an ecological and cultural site inspection. The tools and modules simulate real-world tasks so that you can practise safely before going into the field. By working through the activities, you will learn how to observe, record, and assess environmental and cultural features with confidence.

Learning Outcomes

By completing this learning resource, you will be able to:

  • Explain the purpose of ecological and cultural site inspections.
  • Identify key Tasmanian ecosystems and recognise their features on a map.
  • Recognise cultural heritage sites and understand their importance.
  • Apply relevant legislation and guidelines during inspections.
  • Identify threats such as weeds, erosion, and human impacts.
  • Accurately complete a site inspection report using forms, checkboxes, and dropdowns.
  • Review your own learning through quizzes, flashcards, and revision activities.

The learning journey: Landing Page → Learning Modules → Interactive Activities → Site Inspection Tool → Revision & Quiz → Resources.

Landing Page

The landing page is your home base. From here you can:

  • See your progress bar (how much you’ve completed).
  • Click the tabs across the top to move between sections.
  • Choose a module and begin learning.

Tabs and What They Do

Each tab leads you to a different part of the learning resource:

  • Learning Materials – the main modules. Watch videos, read text, and follow examples.
  • Interactive Activities – practise with maps, identification tools, and threat scenarios.
  • Site Inspection Tool – fill out a realistic inspection form using checkboxes, dropdowns, and text fields.
  • Revision & Quiz – test yourself with quizzes, flashcards, and matching tasks.
  • Resources – download guides and access external websites for extra support.

Learning Modules

Work through the modules in order, starting from the top:

  1. Introduction to Ecological Site Inspection – understand why site inspections are important.
  2. Tasmanian Ecosystems – explore ecosystem types with interactive TasVeg maps.
  3. Cultural Heritage Considerations – learn how to recognise and respect cultural sites.
  4. Legislative Framework – understand the laws and guidelines you must follow.
Tip: Always complete a module fully before moving to the next one.

Interactive Activities

  • Species Identification – match species with names and images.
  • Ecosystem Mapping – zoom and click on interactive TasVeg maps to practise recognising vegetation communities.
  • Threat Identification – use dropdowns and checkboxes to record threats like weeds, erosion, or human impact.

Site Inspection Tool

The tool simulates a real inspection form:

  • Fill in site details using text fields and dropdowns.
  • Select ecosystem types and threats using checkboxes.
  • Record observations in text boxes (vegetation condition, cultural features, risks).
  • Submit your answers when finished to save your inspection.

Revision & Quiz

  • Answer multiple-choice questions to check your knowledge.
  • Practise with flashcards and key terminology.
  • Do the matching activity to reinforce your learning.

Resources

Download useful PDFs and check external websites for extra learning:

  • Site Inspection Checklist
  • TasVeg Classification Guide
  • Cultural Heritage Guidelines
  • Legislative Requirements Summary

Final Advice

Move through the resource in order — landing page first, then tabs left to right. Watch the videos, explore the maps, and practise filling out forms with checkboxes and dropdowns. By the end, you’ll be ready to carry out ecological and cultural site inspections in the field with confidence.
